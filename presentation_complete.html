Let's walk through each step.

Step 1, Acquisition

A raw EEG signal is a continuous waveform

The brain generates continuous electrical voltage over time.

To feed into a model, we must discretize it, take samples at regular intervals (250 Hz = 250 samples per second).

Step 2, Quantization

Each sample is mapped to a discrete level

Raw amplitudes are continuous, we need integers.

We normalize amplitudes with z-score, then divide the range into L = 512 discrete bins. Each sample gets a number 0–511.

Step 3, Tokenization

Quantized levels become tokens, just like words

Each bin number is a token in a vocabulary of 512.

The full EEG channel becomes a sequence of integers, structurally identical to a sentence. The transformer reads it the same way.

Step 4, Self-Supervised Pretraining

The model learns to predict the next token

Training objective
𝓛 = 𝓛CE + 0.1 · 𝓛MSE

No seizure labels needed, the signal teaches itself.

After pretraining, the model has learned the grammar of brain signals: rhythms, waveform shapes, transitions. Fine-tuning then specializes it for seizure prediction.
